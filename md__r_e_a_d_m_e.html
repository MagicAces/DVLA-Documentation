Overview

The goal of the solution was to demonstrate our in-depth understanding of C++ OOP programming paradigm to develop a Vehicle Registration Application for the DVLA. Through the extensive use of classes and other OOP Concepts like Inheritance, Encapsulation and polymorphism, we as a group, were able to tackle the project. Using Doxygen, the documentation for the project was generated. Click here to view.

The challenge

We were to cater for the following scenarios:

  • Officer can register vehicles and assign them numbers
  • Officer can issue license to new drivers
  • Vehicle Registration and Driver License can expire and be renewed

Added Scenarios include;

  • Officer can issue roadworthiness certificate
  • Officer can upgrade licenses.
  • Clients can further contact the DVLA.

Steps

After deriving the classes and the relationships between them, we went a step further to see how we could best exploit OOP concepts to facilitate the project. Subclasses became evident for some of the base classes.

  • License The License class has a TempLicense subclass for temporary driver licenses that are issued to new drivers. The License class itself represents the driver's license card.
  • Vehicle 6 different derived classes all representing the types of Vehicles were created. They were; – PrivateVehicle – CorporateVehicle – CommercialVehicle – Motorcycle – GovernmentVehicle – PoliceVehicle
  • PlateNumber It has a subclass CustomPlateNumber representing custom plate numbers. The PlateNumber is for region-based plate numbers, Government and Police Vehicles

Another realization was the presence of the Abstract class, i.e Vehicle Class due to the presence of a pure virtual function.
